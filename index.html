Dear Jesno

Tap to Begin

I learned today that a poem doesn’t need length or the ceremony of words to make my heart kneel. The shortest poem I know is a name...yours! a single sound that carries more weight than anything I have written, more truth than anything I have tried to understand. I could fill page after page trying to explain you, Yet none of it would come close to the ease with which your name steps into my silence and makes a home there. Funny how a life shifts... one day you think you know the language of longing, the next you realise it begins and ends with the quietest syllables..."your name." Your name... the shortest poem, the nearest star, the thing that asks nothing and still defines everything I know about Love. And now I see that some poems aren’t written, they just arrive and all you can do is whisper them, as if speaking them too loudly... might break the spell they placed on your heart.
– Albin
